Summary

423 Flora Vista is a long-standing single-family home on the Mesa, situated on a 6,100 sf RS-7.5 lot. The plan is to move in and live in the existing house while pursuing a detached ADU addition in the near term, then eventually rebuild the primary residence. The property was recently in a prior development application (PLN2025-00240) that was withdrawn — not because the lot couldn't support it, but because the prior owners found high-end construction costs prohibitive. The lot can accommodate both a primary residence and an ADU under current and forthcoming regulations.

The shed now sits on a built concrete foundation in the rear yard.

New Parkway / Sidewalk Requirement

For new construction: 6" curb + 48" parkway + 72" sidewalk + 6" buffer = 11 ft total from existing curb. Property owner responsibility. Not previously included in the 2025 plan set.

Tier 3 SWMP

Applies to substantial redevelopment. Comments from 2025 review were never resolved. Could be costly per Fernando — bioswales, infiltration, treatment BMPs likely required.

High Fire Hazard Area

Effective 7/31/2025. CBC Chapter 7A construction standards apply to any new build. Zones 0/1/2 landscape requirements. ADU still allowed per Brian Banks. ADU requires 1 uncovered parking space due to fire zone designation.

Rebuild Considerations (Long Term)

Cost Reality

Per Ken Dickson: prior owners received high-end contractor quotes at $1,000–$1,200/sf. For a 2,000–2,400 sf build, that's $2M–$2.9M construction cost alone — which is why they walked.

Mid-tier pricing in SB can be $600–$900/sf depending on builder and finishes.

  • Mat foundation — required per geotech on expansive clay

Ken's Recommendation

Start design from scratch under the new ordinance — don't just dust off the prior plans. The regulatory landscape is changing (new open yard rules, parking reductions, fire code updates), so a fresh approach will yield a better outcome.
